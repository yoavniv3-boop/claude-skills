# Homepage Blueprint — The First Impression

The homepage's job is **not** to sell the product directly. Its job is to answer three questions in under 10 seconds:

1. **What is this store?** (category clarity)
2. **Why should I care?** (differentiation)
3. **Where do I go next?** (clear path)

A homepage that tries to do too much converts worse than one with a clear, opinionated focus.

---

## The Homepage Structure (in order)

### SECTION 1: Announcement Bar (top of every page)

Thin strip at the very top. Used for one rotating message — max 3 messages.

**Good content**:
- "משלוח חינם בהזמנות מעל ₪200"
- "עד 3 תשלומים ללא ריבית"
- "10% הנחה בהרשמה לניוזלטר"

**Bad content**:
- Multi-line marketing speak
- More than 3 rotating messages (they move too fast to read)

**Design**: Contrast with the main brand palette — often black/white inversion. Small text. Closeable.

---

### SECTION 2: Hero — The 3-Second Stop

This single section determines whether visitors stay or bounce. Get this wrong, nothing else matters.

**Structure**:
```
[HEADLINE — short, bold, emotional]
[Subheading — 1 line, explains the "what"]
[Primary CTA button]
[Optional: secondary CTA or trust signal]

[HERO IMAGE OR VIDEO — full width, full height of viewport on mobile]
```

**Layout patterns** (pick one based on brand direction):

**Pattern A — Editorial / Magazine** (for premium, fashion, design brands)
- Large lifestyle photo as background
- Text overlay on left or right third
- Serif headline, generous whitespace
- Minimal CTA — often just text with an underline/arrow, not a button

**Pattern B — Product Hero** (for single-product brands, hero SKU brands)
- Large product photography on one side
- Bold sans-serif headline on the other
- Strong CTA button
- Works especially well with an animated or 3D product shot

**Pattern C — Split Screen** (for lifestyle or dual-audience brands)
- Two 50% columns
- Different images/CTAs for different audiences
- Good when the brand has two distinct hero products/collections

**Pattern D — Full-Bleed Video** (for experience-driven brands)
- 5-15 second silent loop (autoplay muted — users hate sound)
- Text overlay centered
- Works for fashion, food, lifestyle

### Hero copy formulas

**Formula 1: The Promise**
> [עיקר התועלת — מה הלקוח יקבל]
> [משפט אחד על איך]
> [CTA]

Example: *"שיער שמרגיש בריא. תוך שבועיים."* / *"מעצבי שיער פיתחו את הסדרה שרוב הקרמים פשוט מתעלמים מהשורש."* / *"גלה את הסדרה"*

**Formula 2: The Opinion**
> [הצהרה נועזת שהופכת סטטוס קוו]
> [1 משפט שמסביר את העמדה]
> [CTA]

Example: *"רוב הקפה בישראל פשוט לא טרי."* / *"אנחנו קולים כל יום שני. נשלח אליך ביום שלישי."* / *"הזמן את השקית הראשונה"*

**Formula 3: The Invitation**
> [קריאה ישירה לקהל הספציפי]
> [הבטחה]
> [CTA]

Example: *"לרצים שלא רוצים להתפשר."* / *"נעליים שמעוצבות מסביב לדרך שבה הגוף שלך באמת זז."* / *"מצא את הדגם שלך"*

### Hero visual rules

- **Never use stock photos in the hero**. Ever. Use original photography or illustration only.
- **The hero image must show the product or the lifestyle** — not a generic "happy people" shot.
- **Contrast must be high enough for text to be readable** without adding a black overlay (which looks cheap). If the photo doesn't allow for readable overlay text, reshoot or use Pattern B.
- **Load time is critical** — the hero image must be optimized to under 200KB. Use WebP format with JPEG fallback.

### SECTION 3: The Proof Strip (Social Proof, immediately below the fold)

**Purpose**: Within the first scroll, the visitor sees proof that real people buy and trust this store.

**Variant A — Press/Trust Logos** (for brands that have press mentions)
Row of 4-6 logos: "כפי שפורסם ב..." with publication logos (Ynet, Mako, TheMarker, Calcalist, etc.)

**Variant B — Customer Count**
Big number + sentence: *"12,000+ לקוחות מרוצים בישראל"* or *"1 מתוך 3 חברות סטארטאפ בתל אביב משתמשות ב..."*

**Variant C — Review Summary**
Star rating + count + one highlighted review: *"★★★★★ 4.9 מתוך 1,240 ביקורות"* + a rotating customer quote

**Variant D — Instagram/UGC Grid**
4-8 real customer photos from Instagram, clickable. Extremely effective for visual product categories (fashion, beauty, home).

**For new stores with no proof**:
Skip this section entirely. Don't fake it. Move the product showcase up instead. Add this section once you have 3+ real elements.

---

### SECTION 4: Featured Product or Collection

**For single-product or hero-product brands**: one large, beautiful product showcase with a direct "קנה עכשיו" CTA.

**For multi-product brands**: a curated collection grid, 4-8 products max. Not "all products" — a chosen selection.

**Design principles**:
- Product photos should be consistent in style (all on-white, or all lifestyle — don't mix).
- Quick "הוסף לעגלה" button on hover (desktop) or tap-to-add (mobile).
- Hover/tap reveals a second image — a second angle or lifestyle shot.

---

### SECTION 5: The Brand Story (60 seconds max)

**Purpose**: Build emotional connection. Differentiate from competitors who all sell similar products.

**What this is NOT**:
- "Our mission is..."
- "Founded in 2020 with a passion for..."
- Corporate About Us paragraphs

**What this IS**:
- A specific, concrete origin moment or point of view
- 2-3 short paragraphs max, or even better: 1 paragraph + a photo of the founder
- Written in first person if the founder is a real presence in the brand

**Template**:
```
[Headline — the point of view, not the brand name]

[Short paragraph — the specific problem the founder saw]
[Short paragraph — what makes their solution different]
[Optional: single-line promise / commitment]

[Link: קרא את הסיפור המלא →]
```

**Example** (for a skincare brand):
> **"התחלנו כי לא מצאנו קרם שלא ניסה לשקר לנו."**
>
> אחרי 15 שנה בתעשיית הקוסמטיקה, ראיתי מבפנים איך עובד המשחק: תוספים ריקים, הבטחות רפואיות שלא נבדקו, ואריזות שעולות יותר מהתוכן.
>
> אז ישבתי עם פורמולטורית מהולנד ופיתחנו 4 מוצרים. לא 40. כי זה מה שהעור באמת צריך.

### When to skip this section

If the brand is pure dropshipping or generic products — skip. Don't fabricate a story. Use that space for something else (extra trust signals, a FAQ, editorial content).

---

### SECTION 6: Value Props Strip (The 4-Column)

**Purpose**: Communicate the non-negotiable value the store provides, regardless of which product the visitor ends up buying.

**Layout**: 4 columns on desktop, 2x2 grid on mobile.

Each column:
```
[Icon — simple line icon, not emoji]
[Headline — 2-4 words]
[Subtext — 1 line]
```

**Standard set for Israeli stores**:
1. 🚚 **משלוח חינם** / מעל ₪200 לכל הארץ
2. ↩ **החזרה קלה** / 30 יום, בלי שאלות
3. 💬 **תמיכה בעברית** / WhatsApp, כל יום
4. 💳 **עד 3 תשלומים** / ללא ריבית, בכל הזמנה

**Alternative set for premium brands**:
1. ✓ מוצרים ייחודיים בעיצובנו
2. ✓ איכות גבוהה, מוכחת
3. ✓ שירות אישי
4. ✓ אחריות של שנה

---

### SECTION 7: Second Product Showcase / Category Navigation

**Purpose**: Give visitors who scrolled this far a second chance to find something they want.

**For multi-category stores**: show 3-4 category tiles with lifestyle images. Example: "נשים" / "גברים" / "אקססוריז".

**For single-category stores**: show a "נמכרים ביותר" or "חדשים" grid.

**For single-product stores**: show variants or the bundle offer here.

---

### SECTION 8: Testimonials / Review Wall

**Purpose**: Concentrated proof, right before the final CTA.

**Best formats**:

**Format A — Quote Carousel** (for text-heavy testimonials)
Large quote, customer name + photo, star rating. 3-4 testimonials rotating. Manual arrows — NO autoplay.

**Format B — Review Grid** (for review-rich stores)
6-9 real reviews with photos, pulled automatically from a reviews app (Judge.me, Loox). Each card shows: photo, name, star rating, review excerpt, date.

**Format C — Video Testimonials** (for high-ticket or unusual products)
2-4 short video clips (15-30 sec each) of real customers. Extremely high trust. Invest in this if the product is over ₪500.

---

### SECTION 9: Email Capture (Soft Conversion)

**Purpose**: Convert the 90%+ of visitors who won't buy today into marketable contacts.

**Placement**: Near the footer, but not in the footer. Give it its own section.

**Copy formula**:
```
[Headline — the offer, not "הצטרף לניוזלטר"]
[Subtext — what they actually get]
[Email input + CTA]
[Tiny disclaimer]
```

**Good copy**:
> **10% הנחה על ההזמנה הראשונה**
> תרשם לעדכונים ונשלח לך קוד מיד.
> [אימייל___] [קבל את ההנחה]
> *הרשמה אחת, הסרה בקליק.*

**Bad copy**:
> "הצטרפו לניוזלטר שלנו"
> [email]

**Note**: "הצטרף לניוזלטר" is the kiss of death. Nobody wants one more email. They want the discount. Lead with the reward.

---

### SECTION 10: Footer

Underrated. The footer is the second-most-read part of the page after the hero.

**Required columns**:
1. **Brand info**: logo, 1-line mission, social icons
2. **חנות**: links to main collections / featured products
3. **שירות לקוחות**: שאלות ותשובות, משלוחים, החזרות, צור קשר
4. **מידע**: עלינו, תקנון, מדיניות פרטיות, נגישות

**Payment method icons row** at the bottom — communicates trust and choice. Include: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, PayPal, Bit, Apple Pay, Google Pay.

**WhatsApp floating button**: position: fixed, bottom-left (RTL), always visible. This is standard on Israeli stores and significantly improves trust. Message template: *"היי! אפשר לעזור?"*

## Things homepages commonly do wrong

1. **Too many sections** — if there are more than 10 sections, cut. A tight 7-section homepage outperforms a sprawling 14-section one.
2. **Carousel hero** — autoplay carousels hide your best content. Use a single, confident hero.
3. **"Shop all" as the main CTA** — too vague. Direct people to a specific collection or hero product.
4. **Stock photography** — immediately identifies the brand as inauthentic. One poorly-lit real photo beats ten polished stock photos.
5. **Copy that could belong to any brand** — "quality products at great prices" says nothing. Every line of copy should be sayable ONLY about this specific brand.
6. **Walls of text** — if copy doesn't fit in ~40 words per section, cut it.
7. **No clear hierarchy** — if every section shouts equally loud, nothing is heard. Build visual priority: hero biggest, then featured product, then supporting sections quieter.

## Mobile-specific homepage rules

Since 85%+ of Israeli ecommerce traffic is mobile, design mobile-first:

- Hero height: 100vh (full screen) works if the visual is powerful. Otherwise 80vh.
- Product grids on mobile: 2 columns, not 1. Users want to scan faster.
- Never hide the menu behind a hamburger without a clear cart icon visible.
- Announcement bar stays on scroll; header can collapse.
- All tap targets minimum 44px tall (Apple's HIG standard).
- Avoid hover-only interactions — everything must work with tap.
