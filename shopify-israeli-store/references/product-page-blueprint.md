# Product Page Blueprint — The Conversion Engine

The product page is where 70-90% of the purchase decision happens. A great product page can double a store's revenue without any change to traffic. This is the most important file in the skill.

## The Anatomy of a High-Converting PDP (Product Detail Page)

Every section below serves a specific psychological purpose. Build in this order, top to bottom.

---

### SECTION 1: Above-the-Fold — The 5-Second Test

**Psychological purpose**: Answer "what is this, why should I care, can I trust it, how much" in 5 seconds.

**Layout (mobile)**:
```
[Gallery — full width, 4:5 or 1:1 aspect]
[Product title — large, bold]
[Star rating + review count, clickable → scrolls to reviews]
[Price, with "was" price if on sale, + savings %]
[Key USP bullets — 3 max, with icons]
[Variant selector — color/size]
[Quantity + Add to Cart — large, high contrast]
[Trust row: משלוח חינם | החזרה 30 יום | תשלומים]
```

**Layout (desktop)**:
Two columns. Gallery on the right (RTL), info stack on the left. Gallery sticky on scroll.

**Gallery rules**:
- Minimum 5 images. Mix of: lifestyle, product-on-white, scale reference, detail macro, and ideally a short video (5-15 sec).
- First image is the hero — the one that stops the scroll. Usually lifestyle, not on-white.
- Never use generic stock photos. If the user doesn't have photography, tell them to invest in it before launching — this is the single highest-ROI investment in ecommerce.

**Copy template** (fill in based on product):

```
[Product Name — benefit-led, not feature-led]
Example: Not "נעליים מעור איטלקי" but "הנעל שתרצה לנעול כל יום"

[Price line]
₪[price] | במקום ₪[was] | חוסך ₪[diff] (-XX%)

[3 USP bullets — What You Get, not What It Is]
✓ [תועלת 1 — מה היא נותנת לך]
✓ [תועלת 2]
✓ [תועלת 3]

[Trust row microcopy]
🚚 משלוח חינם מ-₪[X]    ↩ החזרה תוך 30 יום    💳 תשלומים ללא ריבית
```

**Why it converts**: The "What You Get" framing triggers the brain's reward centers faster than feature lists. The trust row removes the three biggest purchase objections (shipping cost, commitment risk, upfront cash).

---

### SECTION 2: The Story Strip

**Psychological purpose**: Transition from "is this for me?" to "I want this."

This is a 1-2 sentence emotional hook placed directly below the fold. Not a feature description. Not specs. An **opinion**.

**Copy formula**:
> "[קביעה חזקה על המוצר או על הבעיה שהוא פותר]."
> "[משפט אחד שמסביר למה זה שונה / חשוב / נכון]."

**Examples**:
- For a skincare brand: > "רוב הקרמים נוגעים בסימפטום. זה נוגע בשורש. תוך שבועיים תראי את ההבדל — או תקבלי את הכסף בחזרה."
- For a coffee brand: > "קפה ממש טוב לא צריך הסבר של 200 מילים. תטעם ותבין."
- For a fashion brand: > "נתפרה ידנית, לא מופקת במפעל. לכן היא שורדת 10 שנים, לא 10 כביסות."

**Design direction**: Large serif or display font, lots of whitespace, centered. This is the one place on the page where editorial design is allowed to dominate.

**Why it converts**: People buy based on emotion and justify with logic. This strip sets the emotional anchor before any feature discussion.

---

### SECTION 3: The Problem → Solution Bridge

**Psychological purpose**: Name the pain out loud, then position the product as the only obvious answer.

**Layout**: Two columns side by side (on desktop), or stacked (mobile).

```
Column A: "לפני"
[Small illustration or photo showing frustration]
- נקודת כאב 1
- נקודת כאב 2
- נקודת כאב 3

Column B: "אחרי"
[Photo/illustration showing solution = product]
- הפתרון לכאב 1
- הפתרון לכאב 2
- הפתרון לכאב 3
```

**Why it converts**: When the customer sees their own pain articulated, they feel understood — the #1 prerequisite for trust. The contrast then creates a before/after desire gap.

**When to skip this section**: If the product is pure fashion/aesthetic (jewelry, art). Use a "moodboard" section instead.

---

### SECTION 4: Feature Stack — 3 Hero Features Only

**Psychological purpose**: Prove the promise with concrete details. More is less here — 3 features, explained well, beat 15 listed.

**Layout**: For each feature, alternate left/right on desktop:
```
[Photo/illustration]  [Headline: the benefit]
                      [1-2 sentences: the mechanism]
                      [Optional: small stat or study reference]
```

**Copy formula per feature**:
- Headline = **the benefit** (what it does for the customer), never the feature name alone
- Body = **the proof** (what it's made of, how it works, what the science says)

**Example** (for a pillow):
- ❌ "Memory foam"
- ✅ "התעורר בלי כאב צוואר. בפעם הראשונה מזה שנים."
  - "הזיכרון הפיזיולוגי של הקצף מסתגל לצורה המדויקת של הצוואר שלך תוך 45 שניות, ומשחרר את הלחץ על חוליות הצוואר (C1-C3)."

**Why it converts**: The headline sells the dream, the body sells the credibility.

---

### SECTION 5: The Social Proof Stack (Reviews)

**Psychological purpose**: "Other people like me bought this and are happy." This is the single biggest trust signal in ecommerce. In Israel, specifically, 87%+ of buyers check reviews before purchasing.

**Layout requirements**:
- **Summary at top**: Average star rating (large), number of reviews, distribution bar chart.
- **Filter chips**: "עם תמונות", "5 כוכבים", "4 כוכבים"...
- **Individual reviews**: Name, date, star count, photo (if provided), review text. Verified purchase badge.
- **At least 20 reviews visible** — if the store has fewer, start collecting BEFORE launch.

**Recommended Shopify apps**:
- **Judge.me** — best value, great Hebrew support, ~$15/mo
- **Loox** — photo-focused, expensive but premium look (~$30/mo)
- **Yotpo** — enterprise-level, overkill for small stores

**The "seed review" strategy** (for new stores with no reviews):
Tell the user: before launching, send 20 free products to friends/micro-influencers in exchange for honest written+photo reviews. This is critical. Launching with 0 reviews is 0 conversions.

**Why it converts**: Social proof shortcuts the entire evaluation process. One photo review from a real customer outweighs 500 words of your own copy.

---

### SECTION 6: FAQ — Pre-Handle Every Objection

**Psychological purpose**: Every unasked question is a reason to not buy. Answer them preemptively.

**Standard FAQ structure (minimum 6 questions):**

1. כמה זמן המשלוח ומה עלותו?
2. מה מדיניות ההחזרה?
3. האם הגדלים תואמים לטבלת מידות רגילה? (אם רלוונטי)
4. האם זה מתאים לי אם [קייס ספציפי]?
5. האם יש אחריות? לכמה זמן?
6. איך אני מקבל תמיכה אם יש בעיה?

**Format**: Accordion (expand/collapse). Default state: all closed. Use `<details>` HTML element for clean semantic markup.

**Why it converts**: Removes objections without the friction of contacting support. Also significantly boosts SEO — Google favors pages with FAQ schema.

---

### SECTION 7: Urgency & Scarcity (Honest Only)

**Psychological purpose**: Move the indecisive buyer from "maybe later" to "now."

**Never use**:
- Fake countdown timers that reset
- Fake "only X left" that never changes
- "5 people viewing this" widgets unless real

**Always use** (if true):
- Real stock levels from Shopify inventory
- Real shipping cutoff: "הזמן עד 14:00 — נשלח היום"
- Real seasonal/launch windows

**Implementation**: Place above the Add to Cart, in a subtle tone. Not shouty. A small "⏰ הזמן היום לפני 14:00 וקבל מחר" is enough.

**Why it converts**: Loss aversion. Fear of missing out beats desire to gain — but only when the scarcity is real. Fake scarcity destroys trust.

### SECTION 8: Sticky Add to Cart (Mobile Only)

**Technical**: When the main Add to Cart scrolls out of view on mobile, a slim sticky bar appears at the bottom with: [Small product thumbnail] [Product name] [Price] [Add to Cart button].

**Why it converts**: Mobile users scroll long pages. Without a sticky ATC, they have to scroll all the way back up to buy. This single change can add 10-20% to mobile conversion.

**Liquid snippet** (to add to product template):

```liquid
<div class="sticky-atc" id="stickyAtc">
  <div class="sticky-atc__inner">
    <img src="{{ product.featured_image | image_url: width: 80 }}" alt="{{ product.title }}" class="sticky-atc__img">
    <div class="sticky-atc__info">
      <p class="sticky-atc__title">{{ product.title }}</p>
      <p class="sticky-atc__price">{{ product.price | money }}</p>
    </div>
    <button type="submit" form="product-form-{{ product.id }}" class="sticky-atc__btn">
      הוסף לעגלה
    </button>
  </div>
</div>

<style>
  .sticky-atc {
    position: fixed;
    bottom: 0; left: 0; right: 0;
    background: #fff;
    border-top: 1px solid #eee;
    padding: 12px 16px;
    transform: translateY(100%);
    transition: transform 0.3s ease;
    z-index: 100;
    display: none;
  }
  @media (max-width: 768px) {
    .sticky-atc { display: block; }
    .sticky-atc.visible { transform: translateY(0); }
  }
  .sticky-atc__inner { display: flex; align-items: center; gap: 12px; }
  .sticky-atc__img { width: 48px; height: 48px; object-fit: cover; border-radius: 8px; }
  .sticky-atc__info { flex: 1; }
  .sticky-atc__title { font-size: 13px; font-weight: 600; margin: 0; }
  .sticky-atc__price { font-size: 14px; font-weight: 700; margin: 2px 0 0; }
  .sticky-atc__btn {
    background: #111; color: #fff;
    padding: 12px 20px; border-radius: 999px;
    font-weight: 600; border: none;
  }
</style>

<script>
  const mainAtc = document.querySelector('.product-form__buttons');
  const stickyAtc = document.getElementById('stickyAtc');
  if (mainAtc && stickyAtc) {
    const observer = new IntersectionObserver(([entry]) => {
      stickyAtc.classList.toggle('visible', !entry.isIntersecting);
    });
    observer.observe(mainAtc);
  }
</script>
```

### SECTION 9: Upsell / Cross-sell — "אנשים שקנו את זה, קנו גם..."

**Psychological purpose**: Maximize AOV (Average Order Value). Most stores leave 20-30% of potential revenue on the table by not doing this well.

**Placement options**:
1. **Below the main product info, before reviews** — good
2. **In the cart drawer** — even better
3. **On the cart page before checkout** — best

**Rules**:
- Show 3-4 products max. More is paralysis.
- Real complementary products, not random inventory. If someone buys a lamp, show bulbs — not a couch.
- One-click "הוסף לעגלה" directly from the upsell — no need to visit the product page.

**Recommended apps**:
- **Shopify Search & Discovery** (free, built-in) — basic
- **ReConvert** ($8/mo) — post-purchase upsells specifically
- **Zipify OCU** ($47/mo) — premium, used by top 8-figure stores

---

### SECTION 10: The Final Trust Anchor (Footer of Product Page)

**Psychological purpose**: Last chance to reassure. Place just before the footer proper.

**Three equal columns**:
```
[Icon]                    [Icon]                    [Icon]
משלוח חינם מ-₪[X]          החזרה חינם                 תמיכה אנושית
עד הבית, 2-3 ימי עסקים     30 יום ללא שאלות           WhatsApp כל יום 9-20
```

**Why it converts**: Before the buyer clicks away, remind them that the decision is low-risk. This section should appear on every product page, exactly the same.

## PDP patterns by product type

The skeleton above works for most products. Adjust emphasis based on type:

### Fashion / Apparel
- **Size chart must be prominent** — and in Israeli sizing conventions, with CM conversions.
- Lifestyle photography dominates. Minimum 2 on-model shots per variant.
- Add a "הדוגמנית לובשת: מידה M, גובה 170 ס״מ" note.
- Reviews should emphasize fit: "האם המידה מדויקת?" scale.

### Beauty / Skincare / Supplements
- **Ingredient list must be available** but presented editorially — not as a medical pamphlet.
- Before/after photos are extremely powerful (make sure legal allows for the specific claims).
- Add a "routine" section: how to use it, when, how much.
- Education sells: a 1-minute explainer video on the page.

### Home / Design objects
- Scale matters: always show the product **in a real home**, next to recognizable objects.
- Materials and dimensions go in an expandable section. Don't lead with them.
- Lifestyle is the hero. On-white images are secondary.

### Electronics / Tech
- Specs matter more here. But still, lead with benefits, show specs in an expandable section.
- Demo videos are worth more than any copy. Invest in a 30-90 second product demo.
- Compatibility/requirements must be explicit and scannable.

### Food / Consumables
- Ingredient transparency + "what it doesn't contain" (sugar-free, gluten-free, etc.) if relevant.
- Texture and flavor language: describe what it feels like to eat, not just the nutrition.
- Subscription option (save X% with monthly delivery) — huge for consumables.

## Performance checklist — before declaring a PDP "done"

- [ ] Page loads under 3 seconds on 4G (test in Chrome DevTools → throttled to Slow 4G)
- [ ] All images are served in WebP format with proper `srcset`
- [ ] Sticky ATC works on mobile
- [ ] Review section loads lazily (after above-the-fold content)
- [ ] No layout shift on image load (set `width` and `height` attributes)
- [ ] Works perfectly in RTL (test in Hebrew)
- [ ] Add to Cart triggers a cart drawer, not a page redirect
- [ ] There's a clear free-shipping progress bar in the cart ("עוד ₪40 למשלוח חינם!")
- [ ] Checkout button uses Shop Pay / Apple Pay / Google Pay express options

If all these are checked, the PDP is production-ready.

## Common mistakes that kill conversion

1. **Carousel at the top** — autoplay carousels have been studied to death. They hurt conversion by 30-40%. Use a single hero.
2. **Generic "Add to Cart" copy** — "הוסף לעגלה" is fine, but "אני רוצה את זה" or "קנה עכשיו" test better in Israel.
3. **No reviews** — worse than bad reviews. Collect them before launch.
4. **Hiding shipping costs** — Israelis abandon at 68% when shipping appears late. Show free shipping threshold on product page.
5. **Wall of text** — nobody reads paragraphs on a product page. If the copy doesn't fit in bullets or short sentences, cut it.
6. **Only one photo** — immediate trust killer. Five minimum.
7. **No clear size/variant confirmation** — after selecting size M, the user should *see* it selected clearly. Unclear selection states cause cart abandonment.
